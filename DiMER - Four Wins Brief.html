DiMER Operationalization: The Four Wins

How PHCY 502, 503, and 512 align to strengthen coherence, leverage existing strengths, and clarify each course’s contribution.

The DiMER operationalization across PHCY 502–503–512 creates four mutually reinforcing improvements that leverage what's already working while strengthening cross-course coherence.

Pedagogical coherence

First, we're building on proven pedagogy. The flipped classroom approach that launched our curriculum transformation and has succeeded in PHCY 503 becomes the shared instructional approach across all three courses. This consistency isn't just convenient. Consistency in philosophy and operational details creates opportunities for higher implementation fidelity. Building on early successes and hard-earned lessons learned in PHCY 503 will help elevate the in-class experience as students build on self-paced asynchronous learning. More importantly, when students encounter the same learning structure in pathophysiology, drug action, and pharmaceutics, they can focus their cognitive energy on the content rather than adapting to different instructional models in different courses across a week.

PHCY 502 – Pathophysiology

Second, we're capitalizing on what students already value. Students' end-of-semester evaluations consistently highlight their appreciation for PHCY 502 teaching by clinician instructors. By having these pathophysiology teachers both open and close each week with the patient case, we're exploiting this preference strategically. They introduce the clinical presentation at the start, then return at week's end to integrate the mechanistic, pharmacologic, and delivery details into a coherent picture of how the patient's disease generates their symptoms and how our therapeutic interventions address the underlying problems.

PHCY 503 – Drug Action

Third, we're sharpening the mechanistic focus in drug action instruction. PHCY 503 instructors will teach more deeply into the cellular and molecular features of disease mechanisms that are addressable by pharmacologic activity. This isn't adding new content. Instead, it's redistributing the content more logically. Rather than having disease mechanism live entirely in pathophysiology and drug mechanism live entirely in pharmacology, we're leveraging the Pharmacotherapeutic Hypothesis to create intentional overlap where it matters most: at the interface where broken biology meets therapeutic intervention.

PHCY 512 – Pharmaceutics

Fourth, we're bringing coherence to the pharmaceutics contribution. Instead of front-loading foundational biopharmaceutics content in a standalone section at the semester's beginning — where students struggle to see its clinical relevance — pharmaceutics instructors will teach that foundational content within the context of weekly case applications. The principles of drug delivery, formulation science, and device selection become immediately meaningful when students see how they determine whether a mechanistically sound drug actually reaches its site of action in a specific patient. Moreover, we'll leverage a reusable pharmaceutics reasoning framework for every case: clarifying the target site of action, the barriers to reaching that target, the exposure needed over time, and the clinical fit between product, device, and the realities of patient use. Students will experience pharmaceutics not as a catalog of products, but as a consistent way of thinking about how delivery decisions make or break the therapeutic promise of drug action.
